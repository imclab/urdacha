Theo ~ Exploratory Data Analysis #2

This is my second day as an Urdacha 'Data Wrangler'. Yesterday went so well, that I thought I would continue down the same track: identify three related non-geographic data fields and assign them to X, Y and Z.

After a quick look at the available fields, I picked: passenger_on, passenger_off and passenger_load.

This time I did not stop and try to imagine what the display would look like. I just proceeded with the choice.

I was surprised with the result. Quite different than yesterday's tiny caterpillar crawling up the diagonal.

Today's data looks very much like a wire-frame cube.

Let me explain.

The data is about how many people get on at a stop - which I put on the X-axis, how many people get off the bus at a stop - which I put on the Z--axis and the total passenger load which I put on the vertical Y-axis.

If you ask me: how many people get on or off the bus at any particular stop?

I think my answer - and most everybody else like me would say" "Dunno. Two or three?"

From what I can see - and this is just purely visual impression/no hard numbers - the most likely candidate for the total number of people getting off and on the bus is: zero.

I am just saying what I am seeing. I am probably wrong. Nevertheless there are a great number of stops where nobody gets on or off the bus.

OK, then what is the second most likely number of people getting of and on?

Again: Two or three?

Wrong [maybe]!

The second most likely candidate for people getting on and off the bus is: the entire bus load.

So the cube I see is the vertical Y-axis at ( 0, 0 ) is full of items. This means there is a load but nobody is getting on and off.

The other three corners also have a lot of business: These are where : 1. everybody get on the bus but nobody gets off 2. everybody gets of the bus but nobody gets on 3. everybody gets off the bus *and* everybody gets on the bus

I feel certain that this is the sort data that experienced transportation planners are totally familiar with. And, after all, it is logical that most people get on and off at a main bus station.

Nevertheless seeing the data exposed in this way was a fresh and surprising experience.

So I am pleased that a newb transportation data wrangler can also gain insight...
